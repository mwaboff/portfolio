Intro

I love food. Specifically, I love the experience of creating food and how it has the tendency of bringing people together. This is one of the ways how my German oma and I love to bond. So when the professor for my Database and Web Design Integration course asked us to create a website based off of a hobby, I knew what to do.

I really wanted to challenge myself with this project from a backend perspective. I have used popular MVC frameworks in the past, Django and Ruby on Rails, but I always struggled with fully grasping the design pattern. So I decided to build one myself, without any 3rd party tools.

Previous Designs

In 2014, design was a secondary concern for me. Mobile responsiveness was not even a passing thought, and we can see this directly reflected in my original designs.

Takeaways

1. Always start front-end design with a wireframe and mockup.

2. Flex boxes and CSS variables are life savers.
